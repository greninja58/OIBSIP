Biography

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India. He is a former Indian international cricketer and captain of the Indian national cricket team. Dhoni is regarded as one of the greatest wicket-keeper batsmen in the history of cricket.

He made his international debut in 2004 and quickly rose to prominence with his explosive batting style and exceptional wicket-keeping skills. Dhoni's leadership skills were also commendable, leading India to several victories, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, and the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.

Throughout his career, Dhoni received numerous accolades, including the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award, the Padma Shri, and the ICC ODI Player of the Year award. He is known for his helicopter shot, cool temperament, and ability to finish matches under pressure.

After a glorious career, Dhoni retired from international cricket in August 2020. However, his impact and legacy continue to inspire generations of cricketers and fans.
